IBOK Vaishnavism

Based on the knowledge of Vaishnavism and Vaishnavism is a tradition or one of the largest branch of Hinduism, distinguished from other schools by its worship of Vishnu or his associated avatars, principally as Rama or Krishna, as the original and supreme God. IBOK Vaishnavism is related with Lord Krishna as the Original and Supreme God to believe with the attractive features of seeing him as Lila Avatar. This program includes: spiritual discourse, workshops, great slide shows based on a complete life sketch of Lord Krishna and all other avatars of Lord Vishnu in a series with explanations of divine stories and colorful pictures, and presentations by various artists of IBOK Devotional Music.

Avatars of Lord Vishnu

Ten Avatars of Lord Vishnu within Time Frame:

  1. Matsya Avatar (The Fish) - Satyuga
  2. Kurma Avatar (The tortoise) - Satyuga
  3. Varaha Avatar (The Boar) - Satyuga
  4. Narsimha Avatar (The Human-Lion) - Satyuga
  5. Vamena Avatar (The Dwarf) - Tretayuga
  6. Parshurama Avatar (The Angry Man, Rama with an Axe) - Tretayuga
  7. Rama Avatar (The Perfect man, King of Ayodha) - Tretayuga
  8. Krishna Avatar (The Divine Statesman) - Dwaparyuga
  9. Buddha Avatar (The enlightened One) - Kalyuga

    10. Some others believe Krishna's elder brother, Belarama, was the ninth Avatar.

    The last Avatar (Kalki) is yet to appear, and in many versions of the mythology, the Ninth incarnation is mentioned as lord Buddha. But this is a much later addition, done at a time when the concept of Dashavatar was already fully developed.

  10. Kalki Avatar (The Mighty Warriors) - Kaliyuga
